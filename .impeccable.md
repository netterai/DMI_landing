## Design Context

### Users
B2B mid-market executives at traditional industries (manufacturing, healthcare, logistics, finance) evaluating a data/AI platform vendor. Secondary audience: investors and YC ecosystem readers. They land on this page during a 30–90 second skim — they're operators, not designers, and they're wary of generic "AI startup" pitches.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **confident**, **editorial**, **operator-grade**. The brand is Netter — YC-backed (Spring 2026), positioned as the platform that "switches on your data." Tone is plainspoken and serious, not playful or technical-jargon-heavy. The interface should feel closer to a financial-times feature than a typical YC SaaS landing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: light mode, warm-neutral palette (`#FCFAF7` bg, `#15171C` text). YC orange (`#FF6B00`) used sparingly as accent only.
- **Typography**: Cabinet Grotesk (display + body, 4 weights) — distinctive, editorial, not a reflex font.
- **Layout**: asymmetric editorial hero, large display type, hairline meta strip docked near fold, divider-based lists (no card grids for primary content).
- **Motion**: MOTION_INTENSITY 6 — spring-physics reveals, subtle stagger, magnetic CTA. Nothing bouncy.
- **DESIGN_VARIANCE 8**: deliberately breaks the standard YC SaaS template — single-column hero with watermark on right, list-format Outputs section, divider-row How It Works.
- **Anti-references**: Linear-clone gradient hero, Vercel-style centered CTA stack, glassmorphism cards, dark glow accents, identical 3-card "features" grids, gradient text.

### Design Principles
1. **Editorial over SaaS**: dividers, asymmetry, and large display type beat cards and centered hero stacks.
2. **YC orange is rare**: accent only — pulse on the chip, "Combinator" wordmark, and YC `Y` square. Never used for body, CTAs, or decorative gradients.
3. **Rhythm through varied padding**: each section uses asymmetric `pt-*` / `pb-*` rather than uniform `py-*` to create visual hierarchy.
4. **Information over decoration**: every element earns its place. No sparklines, no decorative blobs, no "vibe" badges.
5. **Operator-readable**: copy is direct ("2 weeks", "None required"), no marketing hedging.
